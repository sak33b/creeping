I Will Not Go Back

July 7th

I have locked the door. I have torn the wallpaper as much as I can reach with my hands. She is out now. I can see her outside creeping in the garden below. I am creeping too along the wall. Around and around following the pattern that is left.

John is shouting. He is knocking at the door. His voice is so far away. Like the sound is from another house entirely. I do not know why they are frightened. After all, everything is clear now. Everything makes sense. The pattern makes sense. The room makes sense. I have come out at last. I can not go back there. I will not go back. Nobody will put me back behind that paper.
